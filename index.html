I am a first year medical student at the University of British Columbia (UBC) and an aspiring clinician-innovator. I hold a BASc (CPEN, '15) and MASc (BME, '17) from UBC as well. I was previously a Research Engineer simultaneously under two labs: the Robotics and Control Lab and the STELLAR Lab. There, I focused on innovation and commercialization in anesthesia, maternal health and urology. I firmly believe in the power of technology to improve the methods of medicine and the lives of patients and healthcare providers. My current research interests include medical image processing, computer vision, mobile application development, and technology-based quality improvement initiatives.

My graduate work, "Intra-operative Ultrasound-based Augmented Reality for Laparoscopic Surgery" was supervised by Prof. Rob Rohling. This was at the intersection of ultrasound imaging, augmented reality, and surgical robotics as applied to kidney cancer surgery. For this work, I won Best Clinical Paper at the 2017 Annual UBC Urology Research Day and Best Paper at the 2017 Augmented Environments for Computer Assisted Interventions (AE-CAI) Workshop.
