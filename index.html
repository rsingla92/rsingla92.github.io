I am a Research Assistant in the Robotics and Control Lab at the University of British Columbia. Here, I am focusing on clinical innovation and commercialization specifically in ultrasound-guided needle interventions. I hold a MASc (Biomedical Engineering, '17) and BASc (Computer Engineering, '15) from the University of British Columbia. I firmly believe in the power of technology to improve the methods of medicine and the lives of others.

My graduate work, "Intra-operative Ultrasound-based Augmented Reality for Laparoscopic Surgery" was under the supervision of Prof. Robert Rohling. This is at the intersection of ultrasound imaging, augmented reality, surgical robotics with applications to improve kidney cancer surgery. An easy-to-read snapshot of this work can be found here. My publications are available here.

In the past, I have been a director for a medical innovation event, radio show host, nano-satellite developer, student team advisor, football coach, club president, conference organizer, and a few other things.
