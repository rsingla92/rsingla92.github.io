I am a MASc student in the Robotics and Control Lab at the University of British Columbia. Here, I am an Engineers in Scrubs fellow, a clinically immersive medical innovation program. I am supervised jointly by Prof. Robert Rohling and Prof. Purang Abolmaesumi. I firmly believe in the power of technology to improve the methods of medicine and the lives of others.

My research is at the intersection of ultrasound imaging, augmented reality, surgical robotics with applications to improve kidney cancer surgery. I look to answer the question of "how can we provide the surgeon with guidance using ultrasound imaging to overcome the difficulties of surgery"? Take a look at some of my publications here.

Currently, I am co-director for Hatching Health, a local medical innovation event. In the past, I have been a radio show host, mini-satellite developer, student team advisor, football coach, club president, conference organizer, mentor, and a few other things.

I'm always interested in new projects, initiatives, or ideas. Feel free to reach out.
