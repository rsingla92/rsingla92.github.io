I am a medical student (Year 1) at the University of British Columbia (UBC). I received a MASc (BME, '17) and BASc (CPEN, '15) from UBC as well. I was previously a Research Engineer in two labs: the Robotics and Control Lab and the STELLAR Lab. There, I focused on innovation and commercialization in anesthesia and urology. I firmly believe in the power of technology to improve the methods of medicine and the lives of others.

My graduate work, "Intra-operative Ultrasound-based Augmented Reality for Laparoscopic Surgery" was supervised by Prof. Rob Rohling. This was at the intersection of ultrasound imaging, augmented reality, and surgical robotics with applications to improve kidney cancer surgery. For this work, I won Best Clinical Paper at the 2017 Annual UBC Urology Research Day and Best Paper at the 2017 Augmented Environments for Computer Assisted Interventions (AE-CAI) Workshop.
